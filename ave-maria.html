Ave Maria
Hail Mary
Ave Maria, gratia plena,
Dominus tecum.
Benedicta tu in mulieribus,
et benedictus fructus ventris tui, Iesus.
Sancta Maria, Mater Dei,
ora pro nobis peccatoribus,
nunc, et in hora mortis nostrae.
Amen.
This part, obviously, means the same in English as it does in Latin. Originally, though, it is from Hebrew, where it is a variation of "amin", or "(to) believe". It is used to indicate that the preceding words (in this case, the prayer) are said with sincere belief.
